About

Implementation & Design

This website was created from scratch. No 3rd party libraries were used. The latest versions of languages and syntax were chosen above older, more backwards compatible alternatives (type-script excepted).

The layout is designed to be the most useful for a web developer, someone who is constantly jumping from one bowser or operating system to another. The organization is deliberately simple (no folders or collapsing groups), allowing the user to see everything all at once. Alternative layouts and functionality may be added, especially for mobile.

Form elements are deliberately not styled. Operating systems create shared widgets that are familiar to their users. I have personally customized multitudes of form elements and or used all the 3rd party solutions at one point or another. My conclusion is that the results do not outweigh the effort nor compromises involved. Default form elements are compact, quick to load, have no learning curve and just work. Every designer's nightmare, I know. :)

Database storage was added to accommodate users who might not have ftp access or have the knowledge to edit a webpage. It's also pretty handy for quick edits. The goal is to make saving a bookmark as simple as possible. Enhancements to come.

The Future

As previously noted, alternative layouts and enhanced editing capability are forthcoming. Notable objectives include:

  • Sessions
  • Inline editing
  • Drag and drop
  • Import bookmark files
  • Export bookmarks to file
  • Multiple simultaneous edits
  • Order preferences
  • Bookmark Sets

My intention is to share the code publicly on GitHub. Ideally a user would install their own instance, password protect the directory, and own all of their data. This may also end up being a service I offer and maintain on a subscription basis.

Enjoy!

Help

No groups were found.

All bookmarks must belong to a group. The first step is to create one.

If you do not see the form (at the top of the page) then click the Create button in the footer to open the form. This help modal may be obstructing the form and or the footer.

The form has 4 main sections, starting from left to right.

  1. Action. Choose create or delete.
  2. Element. Choose group or bookmark.
  3. Detail. Provide details pertaining to the desired action.
  4. Submit. Click the corresponding button to submit the request.

Ensure that the form indicates Create (1), Group (2).

Add a Group Name (3) and then click Create (4).

Now follow the same steps for adding a bookmark.

Click Done to close the form.
